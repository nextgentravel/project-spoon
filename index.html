Self-service booking for government travel



You've been approved to travel - time to book!

Please note that the following only applies to domestic travel. If you are travelling outside of Canada, please refer directly to the National Joint Council Travel Directive. Please bring your government identification when you travel, as you may be asked to produce it if your trip involves government rates.

Accommodations

You're expected to adhere to established city rate limits for commercial accommodations. This rate is for the nightly room rate only, and is not expected to cover taxes and fees. A list of hotels with negotiated rates can be found in the Government of Canada Accommodation Directory, but you may book with the provider of your choice.

Selecting accommodations:
  • Consider location when booking; will additional transportation costs be accrued based on this booking?
  • Is parking included (if needed?)
  • Ask whether a government rate is available
  • Do not select a non-refundable/pre-paid rate
  • Some departments do not want their employees staying in AirBnbs. Ask before booking if you're unsure.
Expense claim considerations:
  • Provide an explanation in your expense report if you're unable to adhere to the posted city rates.
  • Do not charge meals to your room. Pay for any additional costs separately

If you prefer to stay in private accommodations, you will receive an allowance of $50/night.


Transportation
Air travel

Consider all costs when booking air travel. In some cases, it may be more cost-effective to book a higher class if it includes baggage fees. As well, consider a refundable or exchangable fare if the dates of your trip are likely to change (meeting dates changed, etc).

You may book with the service of your choice (direct with a carrier or via an aggregator).


Rail

The standard for rail travel is the next highest class after the full economy class.

You may use government code 700804 to receive an 11% discount purchasing directly from ViaRail. More information is available on the ViaRail corporate discount page.


Rental car

The Government of Canada has established corporate rates with car rental companies. Please use the car search page to look up rates and find the applicable discount code. You may then book with the provider directly.

Expense claim considerations:

  • Provide an explanation in your expense report if you require a different vehicle class.

Paying for your travel

You're encouraged to apply for and use an individual designated travel card.

  • Has a starting limit of $5,000 CDN
  • Does not affect your personal credit limit
  • Offers 60-day interest-free period
  • Can simplify your expense claim reporting
